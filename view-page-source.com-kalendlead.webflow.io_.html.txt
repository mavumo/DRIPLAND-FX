        <h2 data-w-id="7cbefc63-2c23-be76-7584-801ed1614db5" class=
        "section-heading">Features that set us apart</h2>
        <div class="features-flex">
          <div class="featured-block">
            <div class="icon-wrap gradient">
              <div class="icon-black"><img src=
              "https://assets.website-files.com/62af7c24457099cf88aecd03/62af7c244570990cb2aecd22_borrow.png"
              loading="lazy" width="20" alt="" class=
              "feature-image"></div>
            </div>
            <h3 class="feature-heading">Risk-adjusted borrowing
            capacity</h3>
            <p class="paragraph left">Kalend uses a risk-adjusted
            approach that considers both liquidation scenarios
            where borrower's collateral assets decrease in value
            and the borrower's liabilities increase in value. In
            this way, the protocol reduces the amount of bad debts
            protecting the lender’s assets.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="featured-block">
            <div class="icon-wrap gradient">
              <div class="icon-black"><img src=
              "https://assets.website-files.com/62af7c24457099cf88aecd03/62af7c244570999c4eaecd23_discounts.png"
              loading="lazy" width="20" alt="" class=
              "feature-image"></div>
            </div>
            <h3 class="feature-heading">Dynamically adjusted
            discount rate</h3>
            <p class="paragraph left">Kalend allows the discount to
            rise as a function of how under-water a position is.
            This turns a one-shot opportunity into a dutch auction.
            As the discount slowly increases, each would-be
            liquidator must decide whether or not to bid for
            liquidation at the current discount on offer. In this
            way, borrowers will not get the entire position
            liquidated.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="featured-block">
            <div class="icon-wrap gradient">
              <div class="icon-black"><img src=
              "https://assets.website-files.com/62af7c24457099cf88aecd03/62af7c24457099c59faecd24_mixer.png"
              loading="lazy" width="20" alt="" class=
              "feature-image"></div>
            </div>
            <h3 class="feature-heading">Dynamically adjusted close
            factor</h3>
            <p class="paragraph left">Kalend allows liquidators to
            repay up to the amount needed to bring the underwater
            position back. As a result, borrowers who are only
            slightly in violation will have less than half their
            debts repaid during a liquidation, while borrowers who
            are heavily in violation will often have more than half
            their debts repaid during a liquidation.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="featured-block">
            <div class="icon-wrap gradient">
              <div class="icon-black"><img src=
              "https://assets.website-files.com/62af7c24457099cf88aecd03/62af7c24457099f5d7aecd20_stability.png"
              loading="lazy" width="20" alt="" class=
              "feature-image"></div>
            </div>
            <h3 class="feature-heading">Stability pool</h3>
            <p class="paragraph left">Kalend allows lenders to
            support liquidations by providing liquidity to a
            stability pool associated with each lending pool. This
            generates additional yields for lenders and reduces the
            amount of bad debts as it reduces the friction for
            liquidations.</p>
          </div>
        </div>
